What is Climate Change?

Climate change means a significant change in the measures of climate, such as temperature, rainfall, or wind, lasting for an extended period – decades or longer. The Earth's climate has changed many times during the planet's history, but What’s different about this period of the earth’s history is that human activities are significantly contributing to natural climate change through our emissions of greenhouse gases. This interference is resulting in increased air and ocean temperatures, drought, melting ice and snow, rising sea levels, increased rainfall, flooding and other influences. If this continues at the current rate, we not only put in danger every habit, but also human life.


The changes we need are radical and immediate, but we can begin with the very smallest of steps.

“We are at a unique stage in our history. Never before have we had such an awareness of what we are doing to the planet, and never before have we had the power to do something about that. Surely we all have a responsibility to care for our Blue Planet. The future of humanity and indeed, all life on earth, now depends on us.”

Environmentalist: David Attenborough


How Can You Help?
The Sustainable Consumer
Eco-Friendly Habits
Food

Eat more vegetables, more than half of food emissions come from animal products.


Plastics

Before sending plastic packages to recycling, we should think about how we can benefit from them at home. We should extend their lifetimes.


Sustainable Fashion

Choose eco-friendly (natural) materials. Try purchase for quality over quantity.


Habit Change 1

The best way to reduce waste, is to look after and repair what you already have. Giving new life to belongings that could easily be thrown out and replaced, means you divert trash from landfill, and save your money in the process.


Habit Change 2

Second hand is not second best. Use up what already exists by trawling through second hand stores, reaching out to community groups, and shopping at your online second hand stores.


Habit Change 3

Use water more efficiently. Turn off the water when brushing your teeth to conserve. Time your showers. Opt for showers instead of baths. Use your washer and dryer only when you have a full load of laundry.
